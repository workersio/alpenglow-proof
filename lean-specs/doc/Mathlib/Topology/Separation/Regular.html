    Regular, normal, T‚ÇÉ, T‚ÇÑ and T‚ÇÖ spaces #

    This file continues the study of separation properties of topological spaces, focusing on conditions strictly stronger than T‚ÇÇ.

    Main definitions #

    • RegularSpace: A regular space is one where, given any closed C and x ‚àâ C, there are disjoint open sets containing x and C respectively. Such a space is not necessarily Hausdorff.
    • T3Space: A T‚ÇÉ space is a regular T‚ÇÄ space. T‚ÇÉ implies T‚ÇÇ.‚ÇÖ.
    • NormalSpace: A normal space, is one where given two disjoint closed sets, we can find two open sets that separate them. Such a space is not necessarily Hausdorff, even if it is T‚ÇÄ.
    • T4Space: A T‚ÇÑ space is a normal T‚ÇÅ space. T‚ÇÑ implies T‚ÇÉ.
    • CompletelyNormalSpace: A completely normal space is one in which for any two sets s, t such that if both closure s is disjoint with t, and s is disjoint with closure t, then there exist disjoint neighbourhoods of s and t. Embedding.completelyNormalSpace allows us to conclude that this is equivalent to all subspaces being normal. Such a space is not necessarily Hausdorff or regular, even if it is T‚ÇÄ.
    • T5Space: A T‚ÇÖ space is a completely normal T‚ÇÅ space. T‚ÇÖ implies T‚ÇÑ.

    See Mathlib/Topology/Separation/GDelta.lean for the definitions of PerfectlyNormalSpace and T6Space.

    Note that mathlib adopts the modern convention that m ‚â§ n if and only if T_m ‚Üí T_n, but occasionally the literature swaps definitions for e.g. T‚ÇÉ and regular.
